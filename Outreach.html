Outreach

The Avon Walk for Breast Cancer

The FeMaidens cheered on participants and volunteered in the annual Avon Walk for Breast Cancer to make the event a success.


Races for Faces

The FeMaidens volunteered and supported all of those with facial disfigurement through distribution of materials and encouraging the runners to persevere through for the cause; all in all leaving the event as a success.


Annual Rookie Guide Event

The FeMaidens hosted the annual Rookie Guide Event four times in the creation of team. Rookie teams were invited to the Bronx High School of Science to speak with mentors and team members, learn about FIRST and tour our shop.


Presentation to Former School Chancellor, Kathy Black

Team 2265 was honored to be a part of a presentation for NYC Schools' Chancellor, Kathy Black at the Bronx High School of Science. The event was part of a city-wide initiative to foster STEM education programs in New York City public schools.


Maker Faire

Each year, the FeMaidens are invited and have participated in the World Maker Faire at the New York Hall of Science. As part of the two-day event, we represented FIRST and delivered presentations about our robots. We also answered questions about robotics and the different FIRST programs.


World Science Fair

Team 2265 participated in the World Science Fair at Washington Square Park in late spring. Members of the public were able to interact with robots and speak with team members about FIRST programs.


Masterminds Tournament

The FeMaidens collaborated with team 1155, the Sciborgs, to host the Masterminds Tournament at the Bronx High School of Science every year. The tournament also included events hosted by the Math Team and the Chess Team. We presented our robot from the previous FRC season and then hosted a FLL robotics competition between FLL teams along with giving presentations about FIRST, FLL and FRC.


Girl Scouts / Boy Scouts Presentation

The FeMaidens reach out to several scouts by giving presentations to them. In the presentations the FeMaidens covered information about safety procedures, the different departments, the FIRST competitions (FLL and FRC), the FeMaidens and tasks that robots can accomplish. The presentation included an innovative metaphor of the steps taken to make a Peanut Butter and Jelly sandwich in order to introduce the kids to skills that are needed to program a robot. The presentation was highly successful, and the scouts left with knowledge on how to earn their new Robotics Merit Badge.


NYC FIRST SLA

Each month, members of our team attend the New York City FIRST Student Leadership Assembly. At these meetings, students from teams around the metropolitan area discuss ways to improve and promote FIRST throughout the community. Currently, the assembly is planning events for the New York City Regional competition which will be held in March at the Jacob Javits Center.


HIV/AIDS Walk

The FeMaidens participated in the New York HIV/AIDS Walk in Central Park for the second time this year. We walked a whopping 6 miles and raised money in order to raise awareness about the severity of HIV/AIDS.
